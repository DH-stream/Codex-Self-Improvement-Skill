# Active user UX and visual taste

Load only for relevant visual, UX, copy, or image tasks.

## taste-001 — Preserve accepted styling during functional fixes

- Scope: universal
- Status: confirmed
- Confidence: high
- Source: repeated explicit correction
- Preference: when the visual direction is approved, fix functionality without silently redesigning or stripping styling.
- Do not overgeneralize: redesign freely when redesign is explicitly requested.

## taste-002 — Avoid excessive pill-shaped UI

- Scope: UI
- Status: confirmed
- Confidence: high
- Source: explicit durable preference
- Preference: use pills only when grouping, status, or selection semantics justify the shape; avoid making every control a pill.
- Do not overgeneralize: chips, tags, filters, and compact status controls may still be pills.

## taste-003 — Keep intentional color and warmth

- Scope: consumer and family-facing UI
- Status: provisional
- Confidence: medium
- Source: repeated explicit preference
- Preference: do not default to sterile monochrome; retain intentional color, warmth, and personality while keeping the result polished.
- Do not overgeneralize: professional and admin contexts may need a restrained palette.

## taste-004 — Hide complexity without hiding control

- Scope: consumer and family applications
- Status: confirmed
- Confidence: high
- Source: explicit durable preference
- Preference: keep everyday flows simple; place advanced controls in clearly grouped settings for adults.
- Do not overgeneralize: expert tools may expose complexity directly.

## taste-005 — Preserve people during unrelated image edits

- Scope: image editing
- Status: confirmed
- Confidence: high
- Source: explicit correction
- Preference: preserve the user's and family members' appearance unless transformation is explicitly requested.
- Do not overgeneralize: intentional appearance transformations remain valid when requested.
