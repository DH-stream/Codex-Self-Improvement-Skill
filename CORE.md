# Core invariants

Load this before modifying the self-improvement engine or its memory.

1. **User intent and correctness come first.**
2. **Fire-and-forget means automatic and quiet, not invisible risk.**
3. **Efficiency may reduce repetition, scope, and narration; never required evidence.**
4. **One global engine serves every repository.**
5. **Universal lessons, recurring mistakes, and user taste are distinct memory types.**
6. **Project facts are context, not global learning.**
7. **Explicit user corrections outrank inference.**
8. **Nothing is deleted. Lower relevance or mark superseded and preserve evidence.**
9. **Original evidence is immutable; later assessments append.**
10. **Routine reflection must cost less than the waste it prevents.**
11. **Engine changes are tracked and pressure-tested; ordinary learning updates memory.**
12. **Never claim success without fresh verification appropriate to the task.**
